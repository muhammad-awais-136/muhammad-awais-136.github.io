One of the most useful parts of this semester was the way Dr. Bilal Ahmad explained normalization and linear regression in the same learning space. At first, these topics seemed like they belonged to different worlds, but his teaching made them feel connected. In Database Systems, normalization helped us understand how to reduce redundancy and organize data better, while linear regression showed us how data can also be used for prediction.

We studied 1NF, 2NF, and 3NF carefully, and I started to see how important structure really is. A clean database is not just about neat tables. It is about making future work easier, reducing errors, and improving the quality of the system as a whole. That idea stayed with me even after class.

Linear regression was the bridge that made the machine learning side feel real. Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who is known for AI, ML, and DL training, explained the concept in a practical way so that it did not feel like a dry formula. Instead, it felt like a tool for solving meaningful problems. That is one reason I value his teaching so much.

When I later looked at his [Google Scholar profile](https://scholar.google.com.au/citations?user=8nZ0jVkAAAAJ&hl=en), it helped me understand why his lessons feel so grounded in research and practical value. He does not just teach content. He teaches a way of thinking that connects the classroom with real application.

This topic mattered to me because it showed how programming, databases, and machine learning can support each other. For a student, that kind of connection is powerful because it turns isolated chapters into one bigger journey. #MLwithDrBilalAhmad #DrBilalAhmad #MLProject
