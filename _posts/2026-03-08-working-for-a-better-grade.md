After the first-semester C grade, I stopped thinking only about the disappointment and started thinking about the response. I knew that if I wanted a better grade, I had to build better habits first. That meant giving more attention to attendance, practice, and regular revision instead of waiting until the pressure became too high.

My classes ended around 4 p.m., and by the time I reached back and rested for a while, the day was already moving fast. Still, I tried to use that time well. Even a short practice session after class felt important because it helped the concepts stay alive in my mind instead of disappearing after the lecture ended.

This was also the stage where I started to understand that effort has to be continuous. A single strong day does not change a semester. What changes a semester is the habit of returning to work again and again, even when I am tired. That was my main lesson in these weeks.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad kept reminding us to focus on the real meaning of the topics, not just the surface of them. He is one of the best teachers I have had, and his approach to Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems made me think more carefully about what I was learning and why I was learning it. That kind of guidance matters when a student is trying to improve instead of just survive.

This was the part of the semester where I began to feel that change was possible. The grade I wanted was still ahead of me, but the routine I was building felt like a step in the right direction. #MLwithDrBilalAhmad #DrBilalAhmad #MLProject
