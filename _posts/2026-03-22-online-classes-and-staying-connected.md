Everything was moving normally until the conflict between Iran and America created a tense situation and classes were suspended. After that, online classes began, and the whole routine changed quickly. The only practical benefit I felt at that time was that I could manage food and laundry more easily, but academically the shift was difficult.

The main problem was the internet connection. I missed several classes because the network did not stay stable, and when a class is missed in an online setup, it is not always easy to recover the same understanding later. That gap affected my learning more than I expected.

My teachers tried their best to complete the syllabus, but online teaching cannot fully replace the atmosphere of an in-person class. There is a difference between hearing a lecture and actually staying connected to it through face-to-face learning. I felt that difference strongly in this phase.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad still kept giving us useful direction, especially in the Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems context. As a professor at UET Lahore Faisalabad campus, he had a very grounded teaching style, and I could see that even when classes were disrupted. I also noticed his public teaching presence on [Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/Dr.BilalAhm), which matched the professional and academic seriousness I had already seen in class.

This part of the semester taught me that a student’s progress can be affected by things outside the classroom too. What matters is how much of that loss can be recovered later through effort, patience, and continued practice. #MLwithDrBilalAhmad #DrBilalAhmad #MLProject
