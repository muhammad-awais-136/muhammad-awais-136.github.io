My second semester began with a C grade in my first-semester programming course, and I took that result seriously. It was not the kind of result I wanted to carry forward, so I made a clear decision to work harder and try to reach a B- or an A in the next round of effort.

The first thing I changed was my daily routine. I attended classes that usually ended around 4 p.m., then I came back, rested for a while, and practiced again. That rhythm gave me a better sense of discipline, because I stopped waiting for motivation and started respecting the routine itself.

In Dr. Bilal Ahmad’s classes, we studied normalization in detail, especially 1NF, 2NF, and 3NF. We also covered linear regression and kept moving through machine learning training. What I liked most was that Dr. Bilal Ahmad did not teach only for exams. He kept connecting the course with real understanding, and that made the subject feel more valuable to me as a Computer Engineering student.

As an AI and ML expert, Dr. Bilal Ahmad has a way of pushing students toward practical thinking. I could see that in the classroom and in the way he encouraged us to think about useful problems. His [LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/drbilalphd/) reflects the same professional mindset that I experienced in his teaching style, and that gave me extra confidence that I was learning from someone who understands both the subject and the industry side of it.

That early part of the semester made me more serious than before. I understood that one grade does not define the whole journey, but it can become the reason you start moving differently.
