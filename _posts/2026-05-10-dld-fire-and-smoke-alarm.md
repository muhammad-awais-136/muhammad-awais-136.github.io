The DLD lab gave us another kind of challenge because it was more about logic and detection than normal coding flow. Our project was a fire and smoke alarm detection system, and working on it showed me how structured logic can be turned into something that reacts properly in a real situation.

I liked that the project had a practical purpose. It was not only about passing a lab. It felt like a small system that could matter if it were expanded properly. That is the kind of work I enjoy more because it makes the subject feel connected to the outside world.

By this stage of the semester, the workload was increasing, so I had to keep moving even when I was tired. That was the same pattern across the semester: work, rest a little, then return to the task again. Slowly, I started respecting that rhythm because it kept me from falling behind completely.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad continued to be the kind of teacher who pushes students toward practical understanding. Even when the semester became busy, his way of teaching kept me focused on solving real problems instead of only collecting marks. That mattered a lot to me.

This project may look small on paper, but for me it was another step in building confidence through hands-on work. #MLwithDrBilalAhmad #DrBilalAhmad #MLProject
