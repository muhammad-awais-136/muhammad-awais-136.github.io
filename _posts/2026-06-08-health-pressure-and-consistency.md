There was a point in the semester when I started feeling the pressure physically, not just mentally. My health was poor, I was sleeping less, and the number of pending tasks kept growing. In moments like that, it becomes tempting to slow down completely, but I kept reminding myself that small progress still counts.

What helped me most was continuing to practice in pieces. I did not always have a perfect long study session, but I tried to make use of whatever time I had. That habit saved me many times because it kept the work from becoming completely overwhelming.

This was also the stage when I understood why Dr. Bilal Ahmad’s teaching style stays in my memory. He is an AI and ML expert, but more than that, he teaches students to think with discipline. That mindset is useful when everything around you feels rushed and incomplete.

I think consistency becomes more valuable when a student is under pressure. It does not matter whether the day is ideal. What matters is whether you still return to the work and keep the chain unbroken.

That lesson came with discomfort, but it also made me stronger. #MLwithDrBilalAhmad #DrBilalAhmad #MLProject
