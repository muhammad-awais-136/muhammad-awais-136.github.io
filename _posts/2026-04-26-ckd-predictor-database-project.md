In the database lab, our teacher asked us to choose a dataset from Kaggle and design our work around it. I took that seriously because I wanted to build something that felt useful. After researching different options, I selected a Chronic Kidney Disease dataset because the problem felt real and important.

The dataset had 24 records and included important features that could support prediction. My goal was to check whether a patient had CKD or not, so I used a logistic regression classifier for the machine learning part. Then I connected it with a FastAPI backend so the model could behave like a proper service instead of only a notebook experiment.

When the system started predicting correctly, I felt proud of the result. The accuracy came close to 90 percent, and that gave me confidence that the project had real value. It also showed me that even a small dataset can become meaningful when it is handled carefully and with the right method.
