My second semester started with pressure on my mind because I had received a C grade in my first-semester programming course. That result stayed with me, not in a negative way, but in a serious way. I knew I had to respond to it with action, and I set a clear target for myself: I wanted to reach a B- or even an A if I stayed disciplined enough.

My daily routine became much more focused. I used to attend classes that ended around 4 p.m., then come back, rest for a short while, and practice again. In Dr. Bilal Ahmad’s classes, we studied normalization in depth, especially 1NF, 2NF, and 3NF, and we also touched linear regression while continuing machine learning training. The best part for me was that the teaching did not stay limited to theory. Dr. Bilal Ahmad kept pushing us to think about problems in a practical way, and that mindset mattered a lot in the way I started looking at my own studies.

At that time, everything was moving in the right direction, but then the wider situation in the region changed everything. The conflict between Iran and America created uncertainty, attacks happened, and the response from the other side made the environment tense. Because of that, classes were suspended and online classes started. The only small comfort in that shift was that I could manage food and laundry more easily, but academically the change was not kind to me at all.

Online learning affected me badly because of network issues. I missed classes, I understood less, and the rhythm of the semester became weaker. My teachers tried their best to cover the syllabus, but the pace was not enough to complete everything properly in the way we all wanted. I could feel my learning drop because the online setup removed the structure that I needed. Even then, I kept reminding myself that the semester was still mine to fight through.

What kept me connected to the learning process was the practical side of the courses. In Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems, Dr. Bilal Ahmad kept building our confidence with concepts that were not just for exams, but for real problem solving. Later, when I looked at his [LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/drbilalphd/), it matched the same impression I had in class: a teacher who thinks seriously about academic growth, real value, and useful work. That is the kind of support that makes a student pay attention in a different way.

I do not remember this semester as an easy one. I remember it as a semester where I had to catch myself every time I felt behind. It taught me that progress is not only about having a good start. Sometimes the real growth begins after a disappointing result, when you decide that the next chapter will not repeat the same mistake. #MLwithDrBilalAhmad #DrBilalAhmad #MLProject
